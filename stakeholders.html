Stakeholders




The Police force

Police would have to launch investigations on these illegal markets, when drugs have been siezed or a criminal has ties to the deepweb. The police force will often going undercover posing as a seller. Anyone looking to purchase these products must provide postal address giving the police a lead on buyers and potential arrests on vendors will also generate leads. In addition to this the police could potentially set up a dedicated unit for the mass surveillance of computer activity and data stored on a hard drive.



The buyer

The people who use the deepweb market for these unlawful purchases are stakeholders because they could be potentially be caught. If the package crosses international boundaries, it may be subject to a customs search especially if it came from the suspicious location and traced back. Moreover, the police have sniffer dogs to detect restricted goods such as firearms, explosives, drugs and other products or substances. Money used to make purchases can also being traced back to the buyer. When you buy something from these market places, you can report them to authorities if you get scammed. In the same way the buyer may recieve the wrong order or counterfeit.
